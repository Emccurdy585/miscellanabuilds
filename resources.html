Resources

The Lego Community is a vast sea of creativity and skill, and thus resources are abound! However, when you are actually looking for them, it can be a trick to find just what you need. One of my passions is to create and collect resources for builders, aspiring and developed alike, to help make it easier to find just what you need to take your designs to the next level! Feel free to scroll, or if you have a specific thing your looking for, click on the icon below to jump to that section!

Colors

A collection of resources pertaining to color, such as Color Wheels, Generators, and Color Theory

Canva Color Wheel

The Canva Color Wheel is a great tool if you're looking for color combinations. Simply choose a color, then choose what type of color scheme you want and viola! It also includes a quick guide that goes over a majority of color concepts

Lego Color Guide

In this video, Dan the Fan goes into Lego Colors. The first half of the video discusses the lego colors in general. The second half focuses more on how Dan and others use colors in their own builds.

Lego Color Pallette

Bricknerd's 2023 Lego Pallette Guide is a great resource that goes to intense depth on the current color palette, as well as the past. In addition, it has a downloadable pdf that goes through getting the actual color values.

Color Generator

Coolors is a website that can automatically generate color pallettes, which you can start to manipulate until you get exactly what you want. Use the hex codes from Brickner's Color Guide for maximum effect!

Inspiration

Collection-ception of inspiration collections

Character Inspiration Board

This is a Pinterest board dedicated specifically to character art. If you're a character builder looking for some inspiration, look no further! With 2500+ pins spread across 27 boards (and plenty unsorted!), you're bound to find something that will spark your creativity.

Techniques

From landscaping to fonts, here are some of the most useful techniques

Intro to Advanced Tech

Curated by Deepak Shenoy, Towering Brick Creations is a relatively obscure blog. Hopefully that will change, as the blog is ripe with amazing resources. An entire page is dedicated to advanced techniques, so if you're not quite as familiar with some of the janky connections out there right now, give the page a browse!

Font Compilations

In 2017, New Elementary ran a Font contest where people designed Lego fonts. If you're looking for some ideas for your own fonts, this is the perfect place! With over 100 entries spread across 7 volumes, there is bound to be one that is just what you need.

Rockwork Techniques

Rockwork is oftentimes a necessity in many niches that require builds of the outdoors. Getting started can be difficult, and with so many styles, it can be hard to choose. This article by Andreas Lenander on Bricknerd goes through a variety of techniques and includes almost 20 examples!

Characters

Useful resources for those who love to build characters

Eero Okkonen

Eero Okkonen is one of the most prolific character builders in the community. Ripe with clever parts usage and creative designs, their works never disappoint. I would especially recommend their works if you are looking for joint connections or building characters at a larger scale. Just make sure to give your own spin to it!

Jayfa

Winner of Lego Masters AU and a former Iron Build contestant, Jayfa has more than proved their skill in the Lego community. A master of creating both creatures and humanoids, CCBS and System, custom designs and pop culture icons, they are an amazing source of inspiration for character builders.

Character Design

Character Building requires a different mindset compared to more typical themes such as vehicles or landscapes and presents a very different challenge. In this video, Ross Draws discusses 5 tips that create the fundamentals of character design. While it may not discuss it in regards to Lego, the process of designing a character from scratch is very much the same!

Building Chibi

In this two part series, Ted Andes discusses with Javier Soravilla and Mike Dung about building characters in Chibi style. In the first article, Andes goes over the background surrounding Chibi and interviews the aforementioned builders. In part two, the group continues the interview and goes into depth on the techniques often used!

Tyler Clites

Tyler Clites is a Lego Masters winner (US) and a former Iron Builder competitor, and exceptional at building whacky characters. With busts, small figures, "2D" sculptures, and even spaceships, you would be hard-pressed not to find something inspiring! I'd especially recommend their builds if you're looking for high-range posability and clean designs.

Sandro Quattrini

Sandro Quattrini is a master of shaping. Their characters cover a range of designs, from astronauts to animals, so you should be able to find something that peaks your creativity! Their feed is almost a masterclass on NPU (Nice Part Usage) and wild shaping, so if you're looking to make something especially refined they're your guy!

Cyclopic Bricks

Written by Eero Okkonen, Cylopic Bricks goes leagues deep in their building process. With almost every build comes an extensive article going over the design cycle and the various techniques and design choices that went into the project. If you are looking for something more Lego-Character-focused, Cyclopics Bricks is a must-read.

Building Bionicle

Written by Aiden Rexroad, a Bionicle prodigy, this article goes in-depth on building Bionicle. The article begins with the basics of the system, gets into mixing in technic, and ends with adding system and mixels into the, erm, mix. For anyone interested in getting into the Bionicle sphere, I would highly recommend giving the article a read-through!

Photography

Tips for snapping pics when all you do is snap bricks

Brick Central

Brick Central is an RLOC (Recognized Lego Online Community), specializing in Lego Photography. With articles ranging from photography tips, behind-the-scenes, and artist spotlights, they are ripe with information regarding anything Lego Photgraphy related!

Immersive Scenes

In this quick video, Jakob Kaiser goes through the process of shooting an immersive scene. They start by discussing what defines an immersive scene, then go over how they introduce lighting into the build and end with adding practical rain effects. If you're interested in getting a glimpse into the process of shooting an immersive scene, this is the video for you!

Phone Photography

In this video, Jakob Kaiser goes through the process of taking pictures of your MOCs without any fancy camera equipment. If you don't have any professional equipment or expertise, this is the perfect way to still achieve decent pictures!

Sites

Links to useful websites, Discord servers, and YouTube channels

DigiLUG

Digital Building is by no means a small portion of the community. However, few communities consider it a focus or even a major component. DigiLUG is a server dedicated to this community. With plenty of experienced digital builders, if you're looking for more content regarding digital building, it's a perfect place!

PAB Availability

The PAB Availability server was created by a group of AFOLs as a way to catalog what parts were available from the late "Bricks and Pieces." Now, it has become much more! The server has a custom bot that scans Lego Pick-A-Brick and notifies members when parts go in/out of stock. It's also a great place to ask about weird parts or molding questions!

Iron Builder

Iron Builder is a prestigious Lego event hosted multiple times a year. Each round pits a current Iron Builder against a chosen challenger to compete for the seat. The challenge? Create MOCs over two weeks using a pre-determined seed part. In addition, every January brings Iron Forge, which acts as a try-out for the actual competition. The winner is guaranteed the chance to compete in the season!

Lego Math

A collection of resources that make Lego Math a little less like calculus

Cheese Slope Mosaics

One of the oldest and most prominent uses of Lego Mathematics is to create cheese slope mosaics; a technique useful for a variety of details, from carpets to stained glass! In this article, guest writer Katie Walker discusses the mathematics involved, how to frontload the design process, and how to install them into your MOCs!

SNOT

In this 4-part series by Bricknerd, the various methods of SNOT (Studs Not On Top) techniques is discussed.

  • Part 1: SNOT History
  • Part 2: Basic SNOT Techniques
  • Part 3: Advanced SNOT Techniques
  • Part 4: Hinge SNOT Techniques

Sorting

Helpful resources for those who are fighting the Forever War of sorting

Comprehensive Storage Guide

Written by Tom Alphin, this Comprehensive Storage Guide discusses the entire process in getting your sorting system up and off the ground, no matter the size.

Tiago's Sorting Guide

A former Lego designer, Tiago Catarino discusses various storage methods for sorting parts. He goes over multiple methods and their pros/cons, and how he utilizes each of them for his Lego space.

Efficient Sorting

In this article, J. Tooker discusses how to sort more efficiently in order to minimize the amount of time it takes to sort. I adopted a similar method, and it at least feels quicker, though I cannot attest to his math.

Fantasy

For the bit of fantasy builder inside each AFOL

Medieval Floors & Walls

In this video, Dan goes over various methods of designing medieval floors and walls.

Forlorn Empire

A current holder of the Iron Builder Title, it would be hard not to recognize an Eli Willsea (Forlorn Empire) build. Virtually studless, their builds display a high level of professionalism and cleanliness. If you're looking to create more refined fantasy builds, Forlorn Empire is the perfect source of inspiration!

Kit Nugent

Winner of the Summer Joust 2022 Collab category and a former Iron Builder, Kit Nugent has quickly risen in the ranks and proved themselves a young prodigy. From elegant, smooth towers to skewed, fiddly stilt buildings, Kit demonstrates how to utilize textures and styles to convey the environment and mood perfectly.

Stud.io

Resources for the digital building software Stud.io

Stud.io Download

Stud.io is a digital building program available through Bricklink. It is one of the most common digital building platforms and is constantly updated with new parts and features. Plus it's completely free! Click on the image above for more information.

Stud.io Tutorial Series

  • Stud.io Introduction
  • Top 10 Tips/Tricks
  • Stud.io Instructions Tutorial
  • Importing LDD
